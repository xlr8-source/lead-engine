## Product Overview

PayBrix is a digital payment collection and receivables automation platform developed by Sremium.

Its primary purpose is to automate recurring payment collection, customer payment journeys, payment reconciliation, and collections workflows while reducing manual administration.

The platform is particularly positioned for insurance organisations that manage recurring premium payments, although Sremium also describes PayBrix as applicable to subscription-based businesses in multiple sectors.

---

# Core Mission

Help organisations:

- automate payment collection
- reduce manual administration
- improve payment recovery
- simplify reconciliation
- improve customer payment experience
- provide real-time operational visibility

PayBrix should always be positioned as an operational efficiency platform, not simply a payment gateway.

# Problems PayBrix Solves

The product is positioned against problems such as:

- manual payment collection
- labour-intensive collections
- manual reconciliation
- missed follow-up
- payment recovery delays
- renewal bottlenecks
- lack of visibility into collections
- fragmented collections processes
- repetitive administrative work
- customer payment friction

Do NOT assume a prospect experiences these problems.

Treat them as potential discussion topics only.

---

# Typical Discovery Themes

Good discovery questions include:

- How are customer payments currently collected?
- How are recurring payments managed?
- How are payment reminders handled?
- How are failed payments followed up?
- How is payment reconciliation performed?
- How much manual administration exists today?
- How are renewals managed?
- How are customer payment journeys designed?
- What reporting is available to finance teams?
- Are payment communications automated?

Never assume the answers.

# Sales Philosophy

Never claim:

"You have manual reconciliation."

Instead say:

"If payment collection is currently handled manually, PayBrix may help automate that process."

Never claim:

"You struggle with failed payments."

Instead say:

"If reducing payment follow-up effort is a priority, PayBrix provides automated collection workflows."
